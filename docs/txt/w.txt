The ‘w’ command in Linux gives us important information about who is currently using the computer, how much the computer is being used, and what programs are running.
Syntax:
w [options] user [...]

Examples:
This option doesn’t print the header.
w –h

This option will ignore the username while figuring out the current process and cpu times.
w –u


This option will toggle printing the from (remote hostname) field.
w –f
